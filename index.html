Spiraling Out

In this exercise, participants will take turns adding lines to create a spiral shape, originating and circling outward from a small, filled circle in the center of the paper. Using markers and/or pens, participants will pass them along to each other from the left or the right in an assembly line fashion. Participants will sit in a circle a round a paer the size of their choosing (paper size must be a minimum of 8.5" x 11") and take turns adding to the spiral in a clockwise (left) or counterclockwise (right) motion and will use the color marker or pen given to them by the participants from their left or right depending on the rotation direction chosen. Try to keep the rings of the spiral about 0.5"-0.75" apart as the spiral grows.

Participants Needed

2-8 Participants

Materials Needed

  • Markers and/or Pens of various colors

  • Paper (Minimum size of 8.5" x 11")

Instructions:

  1. Before beginning, as a group, decide on the direction the group will take their turns and the order the participants will take their turns and then sit in a circle. For the beginning of the first round ONLY, ALL participants will grab a marker or pen of their choosing. Remaining markers and/or pens will be placed in between the first and last participants.

  2. The first participant to start the spiral will add a small, filled circle in the center of the paper and add the first line to the spiral. After their turn, the paper will then be passed off to the left or the right until all participants have added to the spiral.

  3. At the end of the round, participants will pass their marker or pen off to the left or the right, and the first participant will grab a new marker or pen at random from the pile next to them while the last participant will deposit their marker or pen into the pile. Be reasonable with the lines. Don’t make lines so short they consist of a small tick mark but not so long the line makes up a majority of the ring in the spiral.

  4. The process of passing materials, choosing new marker and/or pen colors and depositing markers and/or pens ideally continues until the paper is filled with rings of the spiral in varying colors and line styles.
